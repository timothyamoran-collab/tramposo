Organising Principle
Your touch does not need fixing — it needs automation. When receiving stops consuming conscious attention, that bandwidth is freed for what actually matters: the overlapping fullback, the CB who stepped up, the gap that just opened. Every session starts with the rebounder. Not as a warm-up — as the core habit that runs through the entire program.
Fundamental Habit · Practised Every Day
The Pre-Touch Routine
This is what separates the player who receives well from the player who receives well and already knows what they will do. The goal is for the sequence to be so automatic it happens without thinking.
1
Scan twice before the pass arrives. Not once. Twice. Left shoulder, right shoulder. Build the picture of the field before touching the ball.
2
Decide your first option before receiving. Not after. The decision arrives with the ball, not after the ball.
3
Your body shape already points toward that option. Body half-turned, hips open to the field. The reception loads the next movement.
4
Execute without hesitating. Doubt is the enemy. If you scanned well, trust what you saw. If the picture changes, your second option was already in your head.

Block A — Fundamentals

Bounce Back — Dropping Pattern
4 min
This establishes the foundation of the dropping movement. Drop toward the rebounder (simulating checking away from the CB toward the midfielder). Receive half-turned — not squared up to the rebounder. One-touch back to the rebounder with good weight: a pass a midfielder could play forward first time, not a sloppy return. Reset, repeat. Simple but the body shape is everything — if you're squared up to the rebounder when you receive, the rep doesn't count.
Setup: Rebounder 4m away. Start 2-3m behind it, drop toward it, receive, bounce back. 45 sec work / 15 sec rest.
Half-turned on receptionWeight on the return passSquared up = rep doesn't count
Back-to-Goal — Four Turns
14 min
The four technical exits from a back-to-goal reception. You face the rebounder, receive with a defender cone behind you (between you and the target), and escape toward the target cone forward. Rotate through all four — spend 3-4 minutes on each, or focus on one or two per session if a particular turn needs more work.

(1) Drag back and turn — drag the ball back with the sole, pivot, accelerate to target.
(2) Fake one way, roll the other — shoulder drop one direction, roll the ball the opposite way with the outside of the foot.
(3) Outside hook — receive and hook the ball wide with the outside of the foot in one motion, body already turning.
(4) Spin — shoulder check first, sell a dummy one way, spin the other side of the defender.
Setup: Rebounder in front. Cone 1-2m behind you (defender). Target cone 5m ahead of you (toward goal). Every turn exits toward the target.

Block B — Link Play Application
Three options from the dropping situation · decide before receiving

Three-Option Release
12 min
This is the full link play situation. You drop to receive from the rebounder (midfielder's pass). Body half-turned. Three targets are available:

Left mini goal — lay off to the overlapping left fullback.
Right mini goal — lay off to the overlapping right fullback.
Forward gate — a gap between two cones 6-8m ahead, simulating the channel your striking partner is running into. This requires a turn first (use any of the four exits from Block A), then a precise pass through the gate.

Scan before passing to the rebounder — decide which option you're going to before the ball arrives. If you're still deciding when you receive it, the rep doesn't count.

Weeks 1-2: Left and right mini goals only. Get the two-option decision automatic first.
Week 3 onwards: Add the forward gate. Now all three options are live.
Setup: Rebounder 4m ahead. Mini goal (two cones) 3-4m to your left — fullback. Mini goal 3-4m to your right — fullback. Two cones as a gate 6-8m ahead, slightly angled — the striker's run. 45 sec work / 15 sec rest.

Weekly Finishing Standard
Target across all finishing work this week: 25–40 shots total. At least 50% from imperfect situations — rebounds, off-balance, wrong foot, moving balls. At least 10 weak-foot finishes. Stop when decision speed drops.

Pressing Block — Mandatory
Every week · no exceptions · curved sprint + variations

Curved Pressing Sprint
8 min
The specific pattern — curved run that cuts off the passing lane to the fullback. Central cone = defender, wide cone = fullback. Curve bends toward the wide cone before arriving at the central cone. Body sideways on arrival, last two steps controlled.

Weeks 1-2: 6 reps, full recovery between each.
Weeks 3-4: 7 reps.
Weeks 5-6: 8 reps.
Phase 3: Two sets of 6 with 90 seconds between sets.
Setup: Central cone (defender) 8-10m ahead. Wide cone (fullback) 3-4m to the side. Your curved run passes between both before arriving at central.
Full effort every repCurve not straightBody sideways on arrivalFull recovery before next rep
Pressing Variations — Rotate Across Weeks
4 min
High press trigger drill — walk toward the central cone slowly, stalking. At a self-decided trigger moment (imagine the defender taking a heavy touch), explode into the curved sprint. The walk simulates the positioning before the press. The trigger must be a specific moment, not just running.

Press and recover — press the cone, arrive controlled, immediately drop back 10m into a blocking position, press again. Simulates the second press if the first doesn't win the ball.

Alternate between these two variations across weeks.
Pick one variation per weekTrigger must be specificRecover as hard as you press

Launch, Control, Finish — Aerial
10 min
Juggle to get comfortable, launch the ball high, run, judge the flight, control at pace, finish. The run is the point — you're simulating receiving a ball over the top or a filtered pass. You have to read the flight while moving, which is the real skill.

Rotate through variations across the 10 minutes:
Chest control — one touch to set, finish.
Thigh control — finish on the second touch.
Let it bounce — control on the half-volley, finish.
Early contact — don't wait for it to settle, finish before you're fully set.

Pick a corner on every finish. Not power — placement and early contact.
Judge the flight while runningControl without breaking stridePick a corner every timeEarly contact beats perfect setup
Ground Ball — Run On and Finish
10 min
Roll or kick the ball forward along the ground at varying pace, chase it down, control without breaking stride, finish. This simulates the balls that escape the midfield at your level — the defender is behind you, the ball is running, you have to judge the pace and finish before the keeper closes you down.

Vary across the 10 minutes:
Fast ball — almost running onto it, minimal control needed.
Dying ball — slowing down, you have to adjust your stride.
Slight angle left — ball running across your body, control across yourself.
Slight angle right — same, other side.

The first touch must keep you moving forward. A touch that stops you dead is a failed rep.

Self-Pass into Box — Run On and Finish
7 min
Two small cone boxes placed inside the penalty area — one to the left of goal, one to the right, roughly at the edge of the six-yard box. Self-pass into one of them, run onto it, one-touch finish low and hard into the opposite corner. Pick a corner before you pass — not after.

The discipline: your first touch must be a finish. Not a control touch, not a setup touch — a finish. If the ball isn't on target, the rep doesn't count regardless of how clean it felt.

Block B — Create Space, Place It
Four amagues · rebounder · pick a corner every time
20min
▼
Four Amagues — 4 Minutes Each
16 min
Receive from the rebounder, execute the amague, feel the yard of space it creates, place it into a corner. Deliberate pace — slow enough to feel what the space gives you, fast enough to be honest. Pick a corner before you receive. Not after.

Fake shot — 4 min
Shape to shoot, let the defender commit to blocking it, shift onto the other foot, place it. The fake only works if it's convincing — really shape to shoot.

Fake to pass — 4 min
Shape to play it wide, defender opens up expecting the pass, shoot into the space they've just vacated. Natural for a second striker who teammates expect to lay it off — use that reputation.

Chop / cut back — 4 min
Receive moving in one direction, cut the ball sharply back across your body with the inside of the foot, shoot off the other foot. Good for creating the weak foot finish.

Stop and go — 4 min
Receive with momentum, kill the ball dead, defender overruns, shoot into the space behind them. Best when you've drawn the defender in with your movement.
Pick a corner before receivingThe move must be convincingFeel the space — don't rush itShoulder drop is your default — these are new
