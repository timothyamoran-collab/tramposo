Organising Principle
Your touch does not need fixing — it needs automation. When receiving stops consuming conscious attention, that bandwidth is freed for what actually matters: the overlapping fullback, the CB who stepped up, the gap that just opened. Every session starts with the rebounder. Not as a warm-up — as the core habit that runs through the entire program.
Fundamental Habit · Practised Every Day
The Pre-Touch Routine
This is what separates the player who receives well from the player who receives well and already knows what they will do. The goal is for the sequence to be so automatic it happens without thinking.
1
Scan twice before the pass arrives. Not once. Twice. Left shoulder, right shoulder. Build the picture of the field before touching the ball.
2
Decide your first option before receiving. Not after. The decision arrives with the ball, not after the ball.
3
Your body shape already points toward that option. Body half-turned, hips open to the field. The reception loads the next movement.
4
Execute without hesitating. Doubt is the enemy. If you scanned well, trust what you saw. If the picture changes, your second option was already in your head.

Rebounder — Open Body Shape Receiving
7 min
Stand 4m from the rebounder, but at an angle — not straight on. Send the ball and receive it half-turned, hips open to the imaginary field, so your first touch can go forward or back without changing body position. Alternate: short continuation touch, field-opening touch, shielding touch. The goal is automatic body shape, not thought-out body shape.
Setup: Floor rebounder. Cone either side at 3m — left option, right option. Decide which cone you're going to BEFORE receiving.

Back-to-Goal Receiving — Four Turns
8 min
Back to rebounder, 3-4m. Send the ball back over your shoulder, receive with back to goal. Rotate through four exits: (1) drag back and turn, (2) fake one way and roll the other, (3) let it run across the body (outside hook), (4) spin — shoulder check, dummy, turn the other side. 2 minutes each. Cone 2m behind simulating a defender.
Setup: Cone behind (imaginary defender). Target cone 5m forward. Every turn leads to that target.

Drop and Spin Sequence
5 min
Start high (top cone). Drop in a checking run toward the rebounder, receive, and use the imaginary defender's momentum to spin toward the target cone 15m forward. Alternate: sometimes receive and lay off (wall pass), spin and arrive at the third cone. The full sequence simulates your most frequent match movement.
Setup: Three cones in a line: you (high), rebounder (mid), target (forward). 45 sec work / 15 sec rest.

Third-Man Run — Triangle
5 min
Three cones in a triangle (~8m sides). Start at A. Pass to B (rebounder or imaginary), immediately make a movement that confuses the imaginary defender (step toward C, then cut behind B), arrive at C and receive the return ball. Sprint. The key: move at the exact moment you release the pass — not after. Vary direction each rep.

Curved Pressing Sprint
8 min
Simulate your real pressing pattern. Cone = defender with the ball. Start 8m back, but not in a straight line — in a curve, to cut off the pass to the fullback. Arrive controlled, body angled. Recover back to start at a jog. 6 reps, one set. ~45 seconds per rep including recovery jog. Progression: add one rep every two weeks. Phase 3: two sets of 6 with 90 seconds rest between.
Setup: Central cone (defender), wide cone (pass to cut off). Your curve runs between the two.

Far Post Arrival
5 min
The angled rebounder simulates the cross. Start from an intermediate position, hold your run, and arrive at the far post just as the ball arrives. Alternate between first-time near post finish and placed finish. The goal is timing — arrive with the ball, not before it.
Setup: Angled rebounder simulating a wide delivery. Goal target 6-8m away.

Chaos Finishing — Second Balls
5 min
Send the ball to the angled rebounder so it bounces unpredictably — at an angle, elevated, with spin. Arrive to the rebound as if it were a goalkeeper parry or a post rebound. Six-yard box rule: zero setup, predator brain on, step forward. No looking up, no adjustment — ball arrives, it goes toward goal by whatever means necessary. Chest, shin, knee, wrong foot — doesn't matter.

Before the match — define three intentions
Don't go to the 8v8 with an empty head or an impossible list. Three concrete things, no more. Examples: (1) scan twice before every reception, (2) move immediately after every pass, (3) actively confuse the CB even when the ball is far away. Write them down if it helps. After the match, how many came through?

The post-match question
After the 8v8, one single question: was I hunting? Not how many goals, not how many touches. Was I hunting? If yes — what activated it? If no — when did I lose it and what happened before? This feeds the following Wednesday's session.

12 min

Six-Yard Box Predator Drill
5 min
Stand just outside the six-yard box. Send the ball to the angled rebounder at unpredictable heights and angles — high, low, spinning, deflecting. Default: step forward, assume it's coming to you, closer to goal than you expect. No setup, no adjustment — just react and finish. Chest redirect, shin, knee, any contact toward goal.
Mental cue: The moment you enter the six-yard box — predator brain on. Game-reading brain off.
